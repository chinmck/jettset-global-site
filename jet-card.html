01 — Private Aviation

Jet Card

Guaranteed access, transparent pricing and greater certainty — structured around how you actually fly.

Private aviation should give you control, not another variable to manage. The Jettset Jet Card secures capacity allocation and agreed hourly pricing for a twelve-month period, without the capital commitment of aircraft ownership or fractional programmes. Each journey is matched to the aircraft category best suited to your route, passengers, schedule and cabin preferences — and when the journey changes, the category can change with it.

Pricing Structure
Prepaid Hours × Hourly Transportation Fee

Your programme is calculated around a prepaid number of flight hours and the agreed hourly fee for the aircraft category selected. Every quotation and statement is presented clearly, so you can see how each journey has been calculated before settlement.

  • Prepaid Hours — your deposit secures capacity allocation and applicable pricing for twelve months.
  • Actual Flight Time — measured take-off to landing, billed in one-tenth-hour increments.
  • Minimum Segments — minimum flight-time rules may apply to protect operational efficiency.
  • Additional Expenses — taxes, airport charges, permits and other operational costs are itemised separately.
Predictable Travel During Peak Periods

Demand rises sharply during major holidays and global events — the programme addresses this in advance.

Jettset publishes the programme calendar annually, including up to 25 Peak Travel Days, up to 10 Blackout Days, clearly defined Super Peak Event surcharges, and advance-notice requirements for designated high-demand periods — all set out ahead of time to support planning, availability and pricing clarity.

A Trusted Global Operator Network

Operators engaged for Jet Card journeys are required to hold a valid Air Operator Certificate, comply with relevant aviation regulations, carry appropriate insurance, and remain subject to ongoing operational review. Operational responsibility remains with the licensed aircraft operator conducting the flight.

Designed For

Is Jet Card Right For You

  • You fly privately on a recurring basis, typically between 25 and 75 hours each year.
  • You value agreed pricing and capacity planning over the uncertainty of ad-hoc charter.
  • You want access to multiple aircraft categories without the capital commitment of ownership.
  • You travel regularly within Europe, the United States or the Middle East.
  • You want a dedicated aviation team that understands your preferences from one journey to the next.

If your flying pattern is occasional, highly irregular or route-specific, on-demand Charter may be the more natural fit. Our team will recommend the structure that genuinely suits the way you travel.
